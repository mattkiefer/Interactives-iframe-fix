Major crime in NYC transit system decreases in 2024 while perception of danger remains.

Counts of major crime in the NYC subway system went down in 2024 for the second year in a row with a 5.4% reduction, according to yearly data from the NYPD.

Source: Adam Gray/Reuters

This number includes incidents of murder, felony assault, robbery and burgery. Despite the downward trend, a recent series of horrifying incidents in the NYC subway has led to a perception of danger in the nation's largest subway station by NYC locals.

A 57-year-old woman was killed last month due to being set on fire while on a subway train in Brooklyn. In another incident, a man was pushed onto subway tracks in front of a train in Manhattan, leaving him critically injured.

"The subways will always be a bellwether for the perception of public safety in New York City."

In response to these events, NYPD Commissioner Jessica Tisch said she ordered an increase in officers patrolling the subway trains and platforms.
